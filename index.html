How to read this

Conservative sizes your lots so that even the single worst adverse move across - logged trades would have stayed inside your risk budget above. It has never been breached by anything in this log, but it's also the smaller, slower size.

Balanced sizes off the 90th-percentile adverse move instead of the worst one — historically, roughly 1 in 10 trades moved further against entry than that. It's meaningfully bigger, with meaningfully more expected profit, at the cost of a real (if smaller) chance that a single bad trade costs more than your stated risk budget.

Neither is a guarantee. A future trade can always move further against you than anything seen so far, and past profit per trade doesn't promise future profit. Treat "conservative" as the size to stay in the game with, and "balanced" as how far to push only once you're comfortable carrying that extra tail risk. This isn't financial advice.
